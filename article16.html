How to Identify and Avoid Crypto Scams

The crypto space attracts innovative projects but also scammers seeking to exploit investors. Be wary of promises of guaranteed returns, anonymous teams, and pressure to invest quickly. Always verify a project's whitepaper, team credentials and community feedback before committing funds.

Phishing emails, fake wallet apps and social media impersonations are common attack vectors. Enable two‑factor authentication on your accounts and double‑check URLs before entering credentials. When in doubt, use trusted sources and hardware wallets to safeguard your assets. Vigilant research and skepticism are your best defenses against fraud.
